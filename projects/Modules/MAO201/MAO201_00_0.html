Seasons and Weather

  • WHAT
  • WHY
  • HOW
    In this module you will:
  • use familiar words and short phrases about seasons, months and weather
  • categorise words based on the types of words they are
  • understand short video clips about weather
  • recognise, understand, and use the Māori names for the main geographical features of New Zealand and the meanings/stories behind them
  • communicate about location
  • present your own short weather report.
    Learning how to talk about the seasons and weather in Māori will help you to communicate about an important daily life topic. In addition, being able to explain some of the Māori names of New Zealand geographical features will give you a better understanding of their importance.
    This module will take approximately eight hours to complete.
    You will be assessed by how well you:
  • answer questions about seasons
  • communicate about weather
  • understand short video clips about weather
  • recognise, understand, and use the Māori names for the main geographical features of New Zealand and the meanings/stories behind them
  • answer questions about location
  • present your own short weather report.

WELCOME TO MODULE FIVE – SEASONS AND WEATHER

Tēnā koe!

In this module you are going to learn about seasons and weather in Māori.

In Lesson Five of the last module you learned about Tāwhirimātea, God of the Wind. Tāwhirimātea disagreed with the separation of his parents Ranginui and Papatūānuku and as a way of expressing his pain he unleashed his anger on the Earth, blowing winds to and fro. However, all aspects of the weather come under his influence, not just the wind. The many descendants of Tāwhirimātea also include the various types of storms, and all the different forms of rain.
